üí°Research Highlights

Focus Prediction and Uncertainty Modeling Empowered by Deep Learning

This research introduces an innovative deep learning framework that not only accurately predicts the focal position in defocused images but, more critically, quantifies the uncertainty of its predictions. By providing a confidence assessment for the model's output, this technology significantly enhances the reliability and robustness of automated optical inspection and imaging systems, addressing the challenge of evaluating result dependability in complex or low signal-to-noise ratio scenarios.

Single-Frame Interferometric Wavefront Reconstruction via Bayesian Neural Networks

This study leverages Bayesian Convolutional Neural Networks (BNN) to achieve high-precision, reliable reconstruction of complex wavefronts from a single-frame lateral shearing interferogram. This approach combines the powerful fitting capabilities of deep learning with the probabilistic reasoning of Bayesian theory, effectively overcoming the reliance of traditional methods on multi-frame data or complex iterations and offering a robust, efficient solution for transient wavefront sensing in dynamic processes.

A Novel Method for Shearing Speckle Interferometry Using Optical Vortices

This work pioneerly integrates Optical Vortices into Digital Shearing Speckle Pattern Interferometry (DSSPI). By leveraging the unique helical phase structure and central phase singularity of optical vortex beams, a novel and highly sensitive method for measuring linear shear is realized. This technique provides a new paradigm with potential for higher precision in fields such as micro-deformation analysis, strain field measurement, and non-destructive testing.
